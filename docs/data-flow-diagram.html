Level 1 — Process Overview (one lane per process)

Level 2 — Sub-Process Decompositions

Each Level 1 process decomposed into its internal sub-steps (n.1, n.2, …). Intermediate caches are temporary stores used within the process; final stores (D0–D7) are the persistent databases.

Level 2.1 — Decomposition of 1.0 Ingestion & Frame Sampling

Camera streams are read, rate-sampled, and published to the Kafka frame topic for downstream ML workers.

Level 2.2 — Decomposition of 2.0 Detection & Tracking

Frames go through GPU preprocessing, YOLO detection, ByteTrack multi-object tracking, and dwell-time accumulation.

Level 2.3 — Decomposition of 3.0 Violation Engine

Tracks are checked against PostGIS geofences, validated against dwell thresholds, logged as violations, and annotated evidence is stored in S3.

Level 2.4 — Decomposition of 4.0 LPR / OCR

A vehicle frame goes through plate localization, deskew/crop normalization, PaddleOCR recognition, and Indian-plate text normalization.

Level 2.5 — Decomposition of 5.0 Congestion Analytics

Detections feed a density counter, occupancy calculator, and flow estimator whose outputs are combined into the 0–100 congestion score written to TimescaleDB.

Level 2.6 — Decomposition of 6.0 Hotspot Prediction

Historical data builds feature vectors, feeds nightly model training, and serves live hotspot probabilities with enforcement recommendations.

Level 2.7 — Decomposition of 7.0 LLM Reporting (Claude)

Structured data is aggregated, a cached system prompt is combined with JSON context, sent to claude-opus-4-8, and the response is grounding-validated before storage.

Level 2.8 — Decomposition of 8.0 Dashboard & API

Covers JWT auth/RBAC, REST query serving (Redis cache), WebSocket live-alert push (Redis pub/sub), and PDF/CSV report export.
